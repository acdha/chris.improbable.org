I don't have a proposal to solve everything, just this one thing

by von

Listen.  Just stop talking.  Stop promising.  Stop worrying.  Stop studying.  Stop triangulating.  Stop making speeches.  Just do this one thing, Mr. President:  Repeal "Don't Ask, Don't Tell."

No one thinks that DADT is working.  No one is defending it.  No one likes it.  I don't like it. Your supporters don't like it.  Lots of Republicans don't like it.  And the bigots don't like it, too:  they want gays out of the military, not silently serving.

What more do you frakin' want?  The public supports a repeal.  Look at this friggin' graph.  A majority of Americans supported allowing gays to serve in the military in 1992, when DADT passed.   DADT was a cowardly move back then.  It's even more cowardly now.  Heck, I think that there's a fighting chance that a McCain adminstration would have repealed DADT by now (McCain is cool with the gays, I'm told, and it makes a Nixon-goes-to-China kinda sense). 

But, I get it.  You've got a lot on your plate.  You're a little worried about the 26% of the country who support DADT (and who won't vote for you anyway ... most are penciling in "Glenn Beck" as a write-in candidate.)  Fine.  Here's your alternative:  unless you're signing a bill that repeals DADT, STOP TALKING ABOUT DADT.  Shut the frak up.  Talk to your fellow Democrats in Congress instead, but do it quietly in the backrooms where legislation gets made.  Because refusing to repeal DADT may be annoying, but your agonizing and triangulating over not repealing DADT is even more annoying.  At least double annoying. Maybe triple annoying, with a side a of patronizing for a bunch of Americans who deserve a great deal better.   

So, that's it:  Put up or shut up. 

Thanks.
