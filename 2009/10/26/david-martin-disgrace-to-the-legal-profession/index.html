David Martin, Disgrace to the Legal Profession

by publius

Via Ta-Nehisi, I saw this amazing Anderson Cooper interview of Todd Willingham's defense attorney, David Martin.  It's enough to make you literally nauseous.  Remember that Martin's zealous defense consisted of getting the babysitter to testify:

The defense had tried to find a fire expert to counter Vasquez and Fogg’s testimony, but the one they contacted concurred with the prosecution. Ultimately, the defense presented only one witness to the jury: the Willinghams’ babysitter, who said she could not believe that Willingham could have killed his children.

In the CNN interview below, he explains that he thought Willingham was guilty because Martin himself had done a backyard science experiment and poured lighter fluid on a piece of carpet and burned it.  To noted forensic expert Martin, it looked the same as the carpet in the Willingham case. 

And so even though he has no expertise in any of this stuff, he's now on TV bashing the report of real scientists (the ones he ignored), and claiming Willingham is clearly guilty.  And remember, he was Willingham's own attorney.

He's so defensive and obnoxious about it that it's pretty clear he's protesting too much.  I'd probably feel that way too if my malpractice had caused an innocent person to die.  And hey, at least he stayed awake during trial.

Anyway, here you go people -- this is the face of criminal defense in Texas.  Remember this guy next time you hear Justice Scalia and pals talk about how competent our underfunded state court criminal justice systems are.  Remember that an entire jurisprudence of deference to state criminal courts is constructed on the foundation of David Martin.

Also, everyone should read this entire post from Burnt Orange Report, which rounds up various disgusted reactions from Texas attorneys.  It also suggests that Martin may be violating his continuing ethical and professional obligations to Willingham.
