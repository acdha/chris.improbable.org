The GOP's Medicare Hypocrisy

by publius

Today's Post has a decent overview of Republicans' about-face on Medicare spending.  Demagoguing Medicare cuts is quickly becoming the GOP's go-to argument.  And it's hard to recall a more brazen and cynical act of hypocrisy.  It's an argument premised entirely on the assumption that the public -- and seniors in particular -- will remain ignorant of the issues. 

To begin, let's recall what Medicare is.  It's a socialized single-payer government-run health coverage program.  And it's wildly popular.  Extending this type of system to the public at large, however, is apparently so controversial that it can't even be proposed.  Further, a watered-down version of this system made available to a fraction of the public is "socialism" and a "government takeover," etc., etc.

Second, a majority of the House GOP recently voted for a budget that would privatize Medicare -- and that's in addition to the party's decades-long hostility to Medicare. 

Third, the bulk of the cuts (i.e., limits on growth) are essentially wasteful unnecessary subsidies to private insurance companies.  It's fitting, though, that the GOP's defense of "Medicare" is in reality a defense of payments to big insurance companies.  I mean, I know these things are to be expected, but I do hope the press illustrates just how cynical these latest tactics are.

Finally, the Post article explains that these attacks would probably be far more potent if interest groups such as hospital lobbies and the AARP got behind them.  I know it's easy to criticize Obama for working hard to get these groups on board despite the policy costs.  But if the AARP were agreeing with the GOP attacks (which they're not), it might well be game set match on health coverage reform.
