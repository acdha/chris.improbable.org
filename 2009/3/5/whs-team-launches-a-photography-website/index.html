WHS team launches a photography website

Sure seems like it's a lot easier to use SmugMug or Flickr - and those have the advantage of not requiring a noisy server at home.

A lot of Microsoft news comes completely out of the blue, but this one can apparently be explained. The members of the Windows Home Server (WHS) team are actually people with lives outside of their day jobs. Apparently they have been attending professional photography events during their free time and were inspired to somehow improve the experience of storing and backing up photographs. According to the team, professional photographers need ways to protect their photos other than manually copying them to media (CDs, DVDs, or external HDDs), using a backup program to copy photos to another HDD, or configuring some outside system (external HDD, network attached storage device, or another computer) on the network to keep a second copy of photos.

So what's their fourth solution? WHS, of course. On the Windows Home Server Team Blog, there's an explanation: "Windows Home Server can simplify this process immensely! Automatic image-based backups, redundant copies of photos stored on separate hard drives, and an easy way to add more storage space are just a few of the benefits." And so, they've launched microsoft.com/windowshomeserver/photos for teaching photographers about WHS and how the OS can help them save their work.
