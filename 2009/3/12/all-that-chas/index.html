All That Chas

by publius

I haven’t been able to weigh in on Chas Freeman (or much of anything lately) because of work, but it was a pretty sorry spectacle. There is, however, one silver lining. By being so hysterical, the anti-Freeman camp implicitly acknowledged the weakness of – and lack of self-confidence in – their arguments.

Generally speaking, people who want to shut down “unacceptable” debate aren’t doing so because they’re super-confident about their positions. Instead, debate stifling is associated with people who are afraid of argument – who fear subjecting their positions to the elements. Just look at Obama’s dinner with Kristol – did Bush ever do anything like that?

The range of acceptable public debate on Israel has become unreasonably narrow – it’s far more robust in Israel itself. And the hyper-sensitive, hysterical attacks on critics of Israel’s current policies simply illustrate that – on some subconscious level – these people recognize that Israel is doing some bad things. Otherwise, there would be no need to stifle debate and bully critics.
