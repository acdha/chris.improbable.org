Rounded Corners 223 – Fork early, fork often

 

Best site of 2008? Github. Before I started using it, I tolerated Git. Now I – gasp – like Git and won’t choose any other source control system. Here’s why:

One thing I’m noticing about GitHub is that it seems to be the first open source community through which multiple forks of the same project feed back to one another.

Open source is about the right to fork and merging of ideas. Here’s a cool visualization of contributions to Rails, watch how the dynamics change, about 5 minutes into it, or when Rails moves over to GitHub.  And don’t forget to check out CommitChat, a tool for keeping track of all conversations across all your forked and watched project.

And so it came to be. The obligatory money quote from The technical story of Muxtape:

The thing that’s so wonderful about using beautiful, appropriate tools is that they become an extension of you, your body, you fingertips, and your mind. They get out of the way and let you directly interact with the problem you are solving. Everyone’s tried to remove a screw without a screwdriver; a task quickly becomes impossible that otherwise would be trivial.

My favorite part, though, is keeping a rewrite on hot standby, but you’ll have toread the post and find out what that’s about.
