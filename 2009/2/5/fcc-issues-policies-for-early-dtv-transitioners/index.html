FCC issues policies for "early" DTV transitioners

The DTV delay may have pushed the official national deadline for the transition to digital broadcast back to June 12, but there are plenty of broadcasters who don't relish the added expense of maintaining both digital and analog signals for an extra four months. But thanks to a compromise provision inserted during drafting of the final version of the delay bill, they won't have to wait: stations that want to go ahead and transition an early can, subject to a set of procedures released by the FCC today.

For those who want to stay on course to transition on the original February 17 date, things are relatively simple, at least if they hustle a bit. Those stations have until Monday (yes, this Monday—four days) to notify the FCC of their plan to keep to the old deadline. Then they've got to run the "equivalent" of 30 days worth of viewer notifications for the following week—including a crawl, if feasible, and a heavy stream of PSAs, after which they can cease analog broadcast on the 17th. Well, unless the FCC decides they can't: the agency is reserving a veto for markets where "all or most" analog stations decide to stick with the original date, since Congress intended give consumers more time to prepare. This creates something of a catch-22, since switching "early" is a lot more attractive for a station if the other broadcasters in the area are switching as well, giving the stragglers an incentive to get set up for digital reception.

For anyone who wants to transition after the 17th, but before the new deadline, the rules get a bit more Fizzbinesque. Between the 18th and March 13, anyone who wants to transition early has to provide the FCC 90 days notice and air 60 days of viewer notifications. Exactly nobody will do this, however, because for transitions on or after my birthday (they remembered!) the "streamlined" 90-days-to-go rules apply, requiring only 30 days of both advance notice and viewer notification. So realistically, stations will either throw something together quickly and stick to the original date, or they'll be switching over on or after March 14.
