Journal of Biology tries speeding up the peer review process

One of the things we've covered over the years at Nobel Intent has been the peer review process, and various attempts to fix the problems with it. Nature conducted an experiment with open peer review, which (unfortunately) only really served to show that scientists were generally uninterested in the idea. Now the Journal of Biology is going to attempt another experiment, once that they hope will cut down the unnecessarily long lag time between submitting a paper and it seeing the light of day.

At the heart of the problem is the fact that, in the biological sciences at least, it's often taking forever to actually get work published. It's becoming quite uncommon to submit work and have it accepted; rather, authors engage in a back-and-forth with the reviewers that can take months and, in many cases, says more about the reviewers than the quality of the submitted manuscript.
