Making Education Miserable

This is why public education can never survive on testing and accountability alone:

Think back on when you were in high school. Odds are you were miserable, and other equally miserable people were trying to make you even more miserable.

Now imagine that all your teachers think they’ll be fired. That is, they know some of them will be, but have no idea whom.  Imagine your principal being demoted and a new administrative staff being brought in who institute ultra-draconian, utterly disproportionate, and capriciously-applied disciplinary policies. These new deans exhibit nothing but contempt for students and teachers alike. Imagine your favorite teachers being first on the chopping block – not because they were bad teachers – quite the contrary – but because they either taught “inessential” subjects like art, or because they spoke out against the changes being made. Imagine that you get pre-emptively suspended from school for trying to protest this.

Imagine how much worse off you’ll be, as a student, as a person.

This was my senior year of high school. And now thanks to Rick Scott and the Florida Legislature, it’s now going to be every school in the state, every year.

From the link:

The bill is quite explicit about who gets merit pay (and how two lousy evaluations mean automatic firing) but kicks the problem of finding the money for those raises down to the school districts. The districts, meanwhile, are contemplating another year of devastating budget cuts and layoffs. With no indication that Florida’s hinky economy will have them rolling in money when the full brunt of the merit pay bill hits in 2014.

This is exactly how you draw excellent teachers into the field.
