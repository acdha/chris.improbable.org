Teabaggers heart Red Coats?

They do think the president should have the powers of a king - maybe it goes deeper than that?

By Thoreau

So, um, leaving aside the glaring factual errors in the claim that Obama grew up in Kenya, and more generally the fact that Obama’s formative years were mostly spent with the white bread side of the family, not the Kenyan side of the family, I am fascinated that Huckabee would say this:

…if you think about it, his perspective as growing up in Kenya with a Kenyan father and grandfather, their view of the Mau Mau Revolution in Kenya is very different than ours because he probably grew up hearing that the British were a bunch of imperialists who persecuted his grandfather.

Um, last I checked, the Republican Party was seized by nostalgia for guys who resisted the British Empire.  Don’t get me wrong, if you want to say nice things about the British Empire, that’s your first amendment right.  In fact, some of our nicest, friendliest neighbors are still technically subjects of the Queen.  But don’t go around celebrating a revolt against the British Crown and then accuse somebody of being anti-American because you assume that he holds a particular view of the British Empire.  (Come to think of it, do we even know what sorts of opinions Obama has on the Brits?)

Or perhaps opposition to the British Empire is only OK if it comes from white people?  If only Crispus Attucks had known this!
