Philip Greenspun's Weblog » How did the New York Times manage to spend $40 million on its pay wall?

In the comments, Philip boasts about photo.net in 2007: "Michael: What would be a reasonable cost to build a system that handles hundreds of millions of page views a year? I built such a system: photo.net (about 700 million page views/year when I was last responsible for it, in 2007). Did we spend $40 million doing it? I don’t think so, since we never had $40 million. Basically as of 2007 we had about $100,000 worth of hardware in one rack. We had one full-time programmer, a part-time contract programmer, a part-time database administration, and a part-time sysadmin."

The part-time programmer was me. I guess I'm happy to see I was part of an organization whose cost-effectiveness Philip boasts about. Though, truth in advertising, I had nothing to with the ability to handle millions of page views -- that was Jin Choi and the various data & system administrators. I was working on modernizing the front-end. I was proud that I automated the modernization effort so that it went quite quickly, in terms of hours worked & cost, but not so proud that I was quite flaky in back in 2007.
