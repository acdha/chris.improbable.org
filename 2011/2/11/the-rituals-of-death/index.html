The rituals of death

This has always baffled me: once you get past outright torture it seems like the "put to death" part of the discussion should be more important than the mechanism. This feels like a coping mechanism to let people maintain some distance from what they're supporting

By Thoreau

I vehemently the death penalty, but I do find many of the rituals around it odd.  The government is very, very good at killing people.  It has entire branches that do it with great abandon around the world.  People in nice uniforms routinely plan and carry out murder via predator drones, cruise missiles, commando raids, and numerous other means.  Cops gun down black men in our streets every day.  And yet, when it comes time to kill a man who has been convicted of a crime (note that being convicted of a crime and committing a crime are often not the same thing), they have to have some sort of elaborate ritual where the death is performed via a machine or drugs, rather than having some guy in a uniform (blue or camouflage, I don’t care) simply give the guy two shots to the back of the head.

So great is the need for some sort of elaborate mechanism with the human killer on the other end of a machine, that they’ve even gone so far as to carry out drug deals of questionable legality to procure the tools of death.
