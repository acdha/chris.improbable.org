King Corn Rules the Land

Interesting second-order economic effect reported by Brian Schiner of the excellent Wagon Wheel Ranch in northern Maryland, in his email newsletter:

All predictions are consistent that food prices are going up. We are a nation that truly runs on corn and with a lower yield in 2010 than 2009 and more of a demand corn prices have gone way up. Even Barley that was 2.50 a bushel 3 months ago is now 4.25. The production of ethanol is increasing the demand for corn which raises the price. Corn is not only used to feed livestock,it seems almost everything in a conventional food store has high fructose syrup which is made from corn.

If your first thought is my first thought, it’s that this must be good for grass-farmers. If you don’t finish your cattle on corn, rising corn prices ought to raise the price of cattle from feedlot operations, narrowing the price disadvantage pastured beef faces versus cornfed beef. But it’s more complicated than that:

How this has effected us is the demand for land to grow corn. Land rent prices have gone way up. Hilly land that is harder to grow crops on has gone from 50 an acre in 2005 to 85 an acre and the flatter land we were paying 65 an acre for in 2005 is now 100 to 110. We do everything we can to keep our prices reasonable but we will have to raise prices around 4% for 2011.

I find this infuriating – @#$&%@*%! corn subsidies get you coming and going – but also interesting, in terms of counterintuitive things I probably should have considered but didn’t.
