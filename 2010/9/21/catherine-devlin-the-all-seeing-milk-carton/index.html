Catherine Devlin: The all-seeing milk carton

Problem: Human trafficking for forced sex work.

Insult added to injury: Internet used as a business tool for sexual exploitation of trafficked persons. Geeks, they're using our toy to do evil.

They just messed with the wrong subculture.

Consider:

1. Some sex industrialists post photos of their victims on various internet sites to connect with customers.

2. Many families in the developing world are trying to recover relatives who have disappeared due to human trafficking. They often have photos of their missing loved ones.

3. Good facial recognition software has been developed.

You weren't smart enough to connect these dots. Fortunately, Brandon Merritt of UC Berkeley was. He has a plan to build a crawler for classified ads and pornographic websites, comparing faces with a database of missing people. That will produce a river of false hits, but it will be good enough for a first pass which can be further screened by human analysis, possibly through use of an amazon/mechanical turk.

Project Milk Carton

How about it, geeks? Brandon is looking for someone to lead developing the technology on this effort - probably someone in the Bay Area (some compensation would be involved). I think the work could be sped up by farming out subtasks to groups of volunteers everywhere. Who's up for it?

I'm planning to attend Southwest Ohio GiveCamp in October, and I would love to be part of a team working on part of the effort. Can anybody join me?

Who can contribute their knowledge of facial recognition software? That will obviously be key to the effort, so picking a good package is crucial.

(Incidentally, this would be the BEST EXCUSE EVER to build a bot that ignores robots.txt.)
