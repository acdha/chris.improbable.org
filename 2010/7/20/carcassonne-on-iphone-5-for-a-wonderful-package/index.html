Carcassonne on iPhone: $5 for a wonderful package

The main drawback: draining the battery.

One nice advantage: you can have multiple games going at once with push messaging and easily switch between them. Handy for the bus.

Many developers try to cram standard games onto the iPhone, and they simply don't work on a touchscreen. The best games take advantage of the hardware instead of trying to turn it into something it isn't. Carcassonne does just about everything right on the iPhone, with a $5 price tag. To put it bluntly: there isn't a flaw to be found here.

The graphics are warm and inviting

The game consists of placing tiles on the board to create roads, cities, and fields, and the scoring system is ably explained in both a tutorial and manual included in this release. You can play with up to four other players, including humans in local multiplayer or differing levels of AI. Playing online is also supported, and I was never more than 30 seconds away from finding a game. A game can take as little as a few minutes if both players are speedy, but more deliberate games with higher levels of strategy are also common; you'd best carve out at some time before challenging another human.

What's so compelling about the game is the attention to detail. The acoustic guitar music is soothing, the voice actor who handles the tutorial sounds welcoming, and the graphics are warm and true to the board game; this is an official release. Every aspect of the game seems to be there to relax you and allow you to simply enjoy the experience. The turn-based nature of the gameplay means lag is not much of an issue when playing online.

The game looks fine on the iPad right now after being stretched, but there will be an update that makes the App universal later this year when the price goes up to $10. Buy now for $5 and the update will be free. The game already supports the Retina display of the iPhone 4 and looks wonderful on that platform.

If you have the means, time, and space... nothing beats the actual physical game. But for $5 and ease of use, this is truly the next best thing; a wonderful diversion that mixes luck with skill.
