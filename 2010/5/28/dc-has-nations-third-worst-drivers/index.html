DC has Nation's Third Worst Drivers

This certainly explains a lot. I'd be happy if we could start with some remedial "Red means stop" education

Based on a study by GMAC insurance

The national average for the test was 76.2 percent, with below 70 considered failing, according to GMAC Insurance. D.C. drivers were third from the bottom, with an average score of 71.9.

Maryland drivers ranked 20th, averaging 78.2 percent, and Virginia drivers were 28th with scores averaging 77.5 percent.

Applying the test results nationally, almost 20 percent of licensed drivers, or about 38 million motorists, "may be unfit for roads" and wouldn't pass a state-issued written exam if taken today, the study said.

About 25 percent of participants admitted to driving while talking on mobile phones, eating and adjusting the radio or selecting songs on an iPod.
