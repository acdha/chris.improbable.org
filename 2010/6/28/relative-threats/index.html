Relative threats

By Thoreau

The feds have arrested 10 people charged with spying for Russia. I do not know the validity of the evidence, so I cannot comment on that.  I can, however, note that these people were charged with crimes in a court of law.  Now, charging people with crimes in an open court of law is perfectly fine if we’re merely talking about people accused of acting at the behest of a nuclear power with a global intelligence network, a navy and airforce, oil, natural resources with which they play politics with neighbors, and a $1.2 trillion GDP.  Sure, they may have some resources behind them, but at least the threat is manageable.  On the other hand, if we’re talking about a small network of guerrilla fighters whose leader lives in caves and whose fighters are armed with at best shoulder-mounted rocket launchers, well, you simply cannot charge them in an open court.  The risks are far too great.
