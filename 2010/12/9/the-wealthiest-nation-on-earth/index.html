The Wealthiest Nation on Earth

by Jacob Davies

Via Mark Thoma, the USDA reports that 42.9 million Americans collected food stamps last month, or 14% of the US population.

In 2001, the number collecting food stamps averaged (pdf) 17.3 million.

Between 2001 and 2010, the US economy grew from $39,773 per-capita to $42,247 per-capita (both in 2010 dollars), or $2,474. The '00s were a terrible, terrible decade for growth, but there was real growth. (By comparison, from 1991 to 2000, per-capita GDP rose by $8,136 per person.)

Despite being a decade with massive tax cuts directed largely to the wealthy and businesses and an administration that wasn't so much "business-friendly" as "business's Special Friend", the percentage of the population using food stamps rose from 6% in 2001 to 14% today.

I don't think that was a very successful exercise. Perhaps we should try something different.
