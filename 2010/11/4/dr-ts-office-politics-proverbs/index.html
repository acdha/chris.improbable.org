Dr. T’s Office Politics Proverbs

By Thoreau

I find myself offering more of these, so I’m going to start a series and collect them:

If you’re usually open with your opinions, nobody will ever suspect that you’re keeping something to yourself.  A loud mouth can be a form of camouflage.

It’s fine to become friends with a co-worker if you work well together and respect each other.  Hiring on the basis of friendship, however, is a sure-fire path to mediocrity.

If everybody gets the exact same reward regardless of how much they work, those who work hard will either “go Galt”, hit the job market, or start plotting.

“Going Galt” at work will only cause you to fit in among those you’re trying to punish.

Plotting will only lead to a bad end, at least if done in haste.

If a useless person decides not to be involved in the project after all, say something polite but celebrate afterward.  Whatever you do, don’t try to change their mind!

If you are assigned to work with a useless person on a task that can afford delay, any time your superior wants a meeting on the project insist that you schedule it for a time when the useless person can be present.  The useless person won’t work any harder, but your superior should get a front-row seat to the mess he assigned.

For some reason, these observations fit nicely with this song.
