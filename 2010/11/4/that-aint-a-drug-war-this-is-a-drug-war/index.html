That ain’t a drug war. THIS is a drug war!

By Thoreau

So, people want a drug war.  Let’s give them a drug war.

I want cops searching motorists in affluent neighborhoods on the flimsiest of pretexts.  I want SWAT raids on frat parties at Stanford and Yale, and “intent to distribute” charges against fraternity presidents found in possession of drugs at a party.  I don’t want celebrities with coke habits going to rehab.  I want them behind bars, same as would happen to a poor black person found with drugs.  I want Congressmen led out in handcuffs for sniffing coke with a stripper, and I want the Congressmen put in federal prison.  I want no-knock raids in rich neighborhoods, and if the rich person’s weed stash is within 1000 feet of a school or playground or whatever the laws are for that sort of thing, I want the extra charges applied.

I want every drug user in San Diego and Orange counties arrested and prosecuted to the fullest extent of the law.  I want rich college kids going to prison and getting permanent black marks on their records.  I want undercover narcs working exclusive resort towns.  People want drug laws?  Fine.  Give them drug laws.  And enforce them to the fullest extent allowed by law.

What part of “illegal” don’t you hypocrites understand?  100 million Americans have admitted to using drugs.  Most of them also support prohibitionist politicians.  Give them what they asked for.
