cutting to the chase

by russell

OK, so the President's bi-partisanFiscal Commission has released a initial draft report on how to reduce the national debt.  Or, not really the whole commission, just the two co-chairs, Bowles and Simpson.

The document contains a number of bold proposals.  By "bold" I mean, guaranteed to enrage an amazingly broad cross-section of the population.  They've left very few stones unturned.

  • Reduce Social Security benefits?  Check.
  • Raise the FICA cap?  Check.
  • Medicare and Medicaid cuts?  Check.
  • VA co-pays?  Check.
  • Eliminate some military weapon systems?  Check.
  • Cut fossil fuel research funds?  Check.

And so on and so on.  They even want to charge admission to the Smithsonian, a measure guaranteed to make a generation of would-be grade-school field trippers cry.

Hard core.

Grover Norquist doesn't like it, and the AFL-CIO's Richard Trumka doesn't like it.

At least they got the bi-partisan part down.

There will, no doubt, be a blizzard of discussion, argument, position-taking, thoughful chin-stroking, and general low-grade pandemonium as folks dig into the details of the report.  But for my money, we  can all save ourselves a lot of time and headaches and cut to the chase by checking out Kevin Drum's analysis in Mother Jones.

Seems like the rest is kinda noise.
