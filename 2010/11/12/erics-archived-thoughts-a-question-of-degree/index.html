Eric's Archived Thoughts: A Question of Degree

I definitely share that conflict, and I'm still leaning in the direction of saying that a smart, motivated person can get by with equivalent experience. Autodidacts do well in this field, both due to the relative immaturity of programming (which often feels as if it's still in the pre-scientific era) and the degree to which the web changes as a speed well in excess of curriculum development.

That said, there's a major caveat in the above ("equivalent experience") which is quite fortunately something which the open source community is perfect for addressing. Getting involved in a serious project or two would be my recommended essential alternative for getting a ton of useful experience and connections which might otherwise be hard to bootstrap, particularly as expectations ramp up.

I recently found myself asked for advice, which feels odd even at the best of times, and this was (it seemed to me) of a particularly serious nature. I’m curious to know what you think is the proper answer.

A few days back, I got e-mail with the following questions:

…in your opinion, how useful is a computer science degree for a career in web development? I’m a second year CS major, and considering dropping out because I don’t see the value in it anymore. It’s just taking away my time from learning and doing what I love most–developing web apps. Will dropping out hurt me later on?
