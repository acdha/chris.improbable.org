The criminal code is not a suicide pact

By Thoreau

A San Diego-area man with a house full of explosives is being charged in a court of law. I don’t see how we can possibly allow this.  I mean, he’s a suspected terrorist, he could be blinking in Morse code!

Oh, wait, he’s white.  OK, never mind, I guess we can hold a regular civilian trial.

EDIT:  Following up, I note that everybody is freaking out over a guy in Oregon who tried to detonate a fake bomb given to him by the FBI.  Now, if he attempted something that he thought would kill by a bunch of people, well, there are laws for that, and I hope that those laws are enforced via the proper procedures.  However, the guy in San Diego, the guy who actually had real bombs that he made all on his own without any help from the FBI, is getting less attention.  Frankly, I’m less worried by an alienated loser who needed help from the FBI to attempt an attack than I am by an alienated loser who actually stockpiled his own bombs.  I don’t care what their respective ideologies, backgrounds, or whatever are.  One of them actually had real explosives that he got on his own, and the other was under the supervision of undercover cops.  It says something about our culture that the less dangerous of the two gets substantially more attention.
