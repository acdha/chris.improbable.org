Freelance opportunities at Ars Technica

Got an article idea burning a hole in your brain? Have a slew of ideas keeping you awake at night? If your passion is writing about technology, we want to hear from you.

Ars Technica currently has openings for freelance assignments in just about every area we cover: IT, gaming, gadgets, tech policy, networking, security, and more. We're looking for long-form, 2,000+ word "evergreen" (not time-sensitive) content or serious shoe-leather reporting on technology issues, and we want your pitches. A few representative examples:

  • A lengthy history of the CPU, from the vacuum tube days to the recent past.
  • For those who were there in the earliest days of the computing revolution: "Greybeards: From punch cards to green screens."
  • How you oversaw a citywide fiber-to-the-home deployment, and what you learned.
  • My life as an indie game developer.
  • New airport body scanners: how do they work and are they safe?
  • The state of the Linux graphics stack.

You'll need either 1) proven writing chops at established publications or 2) serious subject matter expertise (be sure to let us know how you cut your teeth). Subject matter experts, bloggers, journalists, IT gurus, you're all invited.

If interested, please e-mail the following information to freelance@arstechnica.com, with the subject line: "Freelancing at Ars."

  • A brief paragraph summarizing your interest and experience
  • A copy of your CV or resume, if you have one
  • A writing sample (or links to content online)
  • One to three specific article pitches, with a brief paragraph explaining how you'd develop each piece.

We will review everything sent to us, but due to the usual volume of responses, we unfortunately can't promise personal replies to everyone. Thanks!
