Where the semantic web stumbled, linked data will succeed - O'Reilly Radar

This has been the main drawback to this entire field: doing it "right" requires a ton of new tech, changes throughout the entire stack, much of which is immature, etc. with the hope that at some point it'd be worth having done so.

Microformat-style (what I'd term web-like) approaches, in contrast, require very little work and begin paying off immediately if you care about Google. That's the only way anything is going to hit broad adoption.

Linked data can be realized without the purity of semantic annotation, but a focus on consumers gives it a better shot at adoption. It begs the question: Why invest in difficult technologies if consumer outcomes can be realized with current tools and knowledge?
