Conference Video Publishing Economics

Steve Scott (Scotty) / @macdevnet:

It appears my bandwidth bill for the next 24 months is going to be about $25000….that’s a bit scary

The pricing does get extreme, which is why I post C4’s videos to Viddler.

As I mentioned before:

Mucho thanks to Viddler for the rockin’ site and the tons of bandwidth. I priced out the costs of vending these videos on Amazon’s S3 — renowned for its low prices — and came up with a $12,000 bandwidth bill. Yeowch.

Trivia: back in the C4[0] days I considered selling DVDs of the presentations, funneling the expected resultant massive profits into buying three additional wheels of encrusted baked brie for our crackers for our Saturday afternoon break.

However I decided that I’d rather not pursue an avenue that would require me setting up a burning+mailing DVD production line or farming it out, and now C4 is forever relegated to the indie developer conference slums with only two wheels of encrusted baked brie for our crackers.

Sorry, fellas.

Turns out making the videos publicly available for free was a bit of marketing genius I wish I could claim to have originated.

Lots of folks who missed C4 have told me in person that they loved the videos and have decided they want to attend based on them.

So distinguished fellow conference-putter-oners, I encourage you to make your conference videos freely available on the Net and let some Web-2.0-shop foot the huge bandwidth bill — it’s great marketing for you.
