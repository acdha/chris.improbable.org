Ars at Yearly Kos '07

If you follow the online political blogging scene, you're probably aware that Chicago will be hosting this year's Yearly Kos convention, a confab of left-wing activists, bloggers, media types, and politicos that's sponsored and organized under the auspices of the popular lefty blog, the Daily Kos. Since I'm based in Chicago, and since I've been covering the intersection of technology and politics on this site since I was a naive, 22-year-old grad student who used Frontpage '98 to post exhuberant rants about the politically transformative power of e-mail in poor African countries, you bet your life I'm going to be there to see what online activism looks like now that it has reached its adolescent phase.

And since the top brass of the Democratic congressional and party leadership is going be there, I'm also going to get a good, up-close look at the new boss to see if he's the same as the old boss, or even the old old bossâ€”y'know, the one who first awakened the aforementioned 22-year-old to the growing power of the surveillance state with programs like ECHELON and Carnivore; the guy who I condemned for abusing executive orders and pushing an unprecedented and dangerous expansion of executive power... ah, that all seems so quaint now, it almost makes me smile, but back then I thought Bill was the first horseman of the Black Helicopter Apocalypse.
