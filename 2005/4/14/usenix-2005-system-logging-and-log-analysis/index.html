USENIX 2005: System Logging and Log Analysis

The general focus is really what Marcus terms “Artificial Ignorance”, a phrase deliberately designed not to be hijaacked by marketing departments: iteratively add ignore rules for things you identify as boring and pay attention to everything else; far less expensive than building signatures for every bad thing which you might encounter. Doing this at scale requires a distributed approach where systems report to aggregators which condense things somewhat before forwarding them on to your central server. Since this is a volume text processing operation it's best to keep things as text files outside of certain cases where data can be usefully normalized (e.g. process accounting, netflows).
