2005 - A South Island Odyssey: My God, it's full of sheep!

A 1.2Mm Holiday

Map courtesy of backpack-newzealand.com:

Good fortune indeed: 14 days on the bike, 1206km on the odometer, ~30 minutes in the rain and no equipment failures or accidents!

Pictures

Route

  1. Thursday, February 17, 2005: Arrive in Christchurch, collect the bike and ride around a bit to dial in all of the settings. Hagley Park was extremely comfortable and had a decent live band performing near the botanicalgardens.
  2. Sunday: Bus from Christchurch to Tarras; Tarras to Wanaka (28km of strong, perfectly-opposed headwinds)
  3. Monday: Wanaka to Makaroa (54km) - a few good climbs and a rather nice transition in the scenery when passing through The Neck between Lake Hawea and Lake Wanaka provided a promising introduction for what the next couple weeks had in store.
  4. Tuesday: Makaroa to Haast (81km) - an exhilarating 500m descent and a couple hours riding through some beautiful rainforest out to Haast. A quick trip over to Haast Beach (11km) provided an excellent stress test for my sandfly repellent and an opportunity to swim in the Tasman Sea.
  5. Wednesday: Haast to Fox Glacier (120km) - a simple cruise up the coast passing through various lakes and beaches on the way into Fox. Over the course of the afternoon we discussed the only remaining hill climb; after all of the build up we arrived in town before realizing that the "hard" climb had been so gradual that we hadn't noticed it...
  6. Thursday: Day of rest. Unfortunately the overcast weather made glacier flights impractical - the few which weren't cancelled apparently had lousy views - and so we went to Lake Matheson and walked out to FoxGlacier instead.
  7. Friday: Fox Glacier to Harihari (85km) - a lunch break in Whataroa produced the only bathroom-graffiti sighting of the trip: a "100% effective sandfly repellent" recipe.
  8. Saturday: Harihari to Hokitika (72km) - lunch in Ross provided entertainment in the form of sitcom-worthy service; a short spin brought us to Hokitika, containing Indian food (a welcome break from English fare) and a bookseller stocking Pedaller's Paradise - a cheeky alternative to the Lonely Planet cycling guidebook.
  9. Sunday: Hokitika to Punakaiki (85km) - fast cruise through the aptly-named Greymouth up the coast to Punakaiki. This was the only significant period of bad weather and the sudden boost in speed provided by the rain kept that down to about 20 minutes.
  10. Monday: Punakaiki to Westport (56km) - a good ride up the coast to Westport, a former mining town offering all sorts of services to passing cyclists. I took the opportunity replace my by-now sadly worn bike shorts but passed on the offer to smoke up with a local tuna fisherman...
  11. Tuesday: White-water Rafting; Murchison to Glenhope (44km) - slept at the Hu Ha Bikepackers, a comfortable cyclists' hostel on a local farm.
  12. Wednesday: Glenhope to slightly outside Nelson (~100km) - a couple fast climbs followed by long (20km+ ea!) downhills through the farms into the increasingly built-up area surrounding Nelson.
  13. Thursday: Nelson to Picton (109km) - a healthy climb immediately outside of Nelson (highlights of the descent included a few surprise patches of unsealed pavement, narrowly passing a steamroller and pick-axe-wielding workers, and a guy in a van matching speed: "Hey man, you're going 65!" and a quick thumbs up). We stopped in Havelock for lunch and headed onto the Queen CharlotteScenic Drive.
  14. Friday: Picton to Kaikoura (174km) - stopped in briefly at the Pedaller's Rest Cyclists' Hostel to procure a nice, locally-made Merino wool jersey; while it looked like a very pleasant place to stay the powerful tailwind made it impossible not to keep cruising.
  15. Saturday: Kaikoura to Waipara (120km) - Friday's tailwind quickly turned into Saturday's nasty headwind and after 90km of pedaling even on the downhills I stayed at the Waipara Sleepers.
  16. Sunday: Waipara to Christchurch (58km) - downhill + tailwind = fun! A stop at a farmer's stand just past Woodend turned up some amazing plums which I had for lunch by the Avon River, close to the weekend lantern festival.
  17. Monday: Scenic plane flight around Mount Cook
  18. Tuesday: Reluctant return home
