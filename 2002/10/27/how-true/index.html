How true

Nick Denton's comment about the Russian government's handling of the hostage crisis reminded me of a constant amusement I've had while reading The Opium Wars. Terms like "imperialism" or "gunboat diplomacy" were traditionally used to mean something like the British selling illegal drugs to solve their trade imbalance, toppling your government when you tried to stop them and then billing you to pay for expenses incurred while taking over your country.

I can't help thinking that it's a sign of progress that we're now more likely to hear them bandied around in response to teenagers giving up traditional clothing and entertainment in favor of Western trends and that it's basically assumed that any military action will be followed by aid and rebuilding rather than the rapine and yearly tribute which was customary until around a century ago.
