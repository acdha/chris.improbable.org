Bait and Switch

Kevin Fox made a hilarious parody of the Apple Switch commercials.

Apple has really botched the .Mac migration. The service is now overpriced - extremely tight undocumented bandwidth caps, a buggy spam filter which silently drops email and general reliability problems. Now you can pay $100 per year and you'll get virus software to combat non-existent OS X viruses and backup software which needs a network connection to backup your hard drive to CD/DVD and doesn't appear to handle backups larger than one disc (early reports say it fails and I'm not going to pay $50 to confirm this).

Alternately, you could sign up with a place like qwk.net and get a better deal. More bandwidth, more email addresses, PHP / CGI, databases, SSL support, SSH access and uptime guarantees.

The main thing .Mac has going for it is that Command-Option-I will open your iDisk in the finder. It'd probably be fairly easy to produce a hack to use another webdav server and it'd be really nice if someone made an SFTP driver like the ftp support which is supposed to be coming in 10.2.
