Shooting the messenger

The Royal Family's security detail is a bit behind the times - they're still sending sensitive information over unencrypted radio links. This is a major security problem but they're in full denial:

An intelligence source said Mr Wey was a "menace", whose actions could help terrorists commit atrocities and may have already been used to counter police operations.

Liberal Democrat home affairs spokesman Simon Hughes said the government must consider banning radio scanners, which are currently illegal to use but not to own.

That's right, the problem isn't security people whose tradecraft would have qualified as criminally negligible in WWII, let alone 2002 - it's obviously the people who pointed this out. A domestic scanner ban makes so much more sense than borrowing some secure radios from the military, too - after all, foreign terrorists would never think to smuggle a radio into the country along with their Uzis and plastique.

The best part: this was first pointed out six years ago! By now, if you use a CDMA or GSM cell phone, your travel plans are more secure than the Royal Family's.
